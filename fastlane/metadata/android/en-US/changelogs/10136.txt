• Reply-to now accepts BOTH wire formats on inbound: Columba's
  `fields[16].reply_to` and MeshChatX's compact `fields[0x30]`
  (raw hash bytes) + optional `fields[0x31]` (UTF-8 quoted
  content). Replies from either client render with a quote
  preview, and a fallback "Replying to a message…" still shows
  when the target hasn't arrived locally yet.
• Outbound replies now ship MeshChatX's compact shape — ~32 wire
  bytes per reply vs Columba's ~80 bytes. Same data, less radio
  time. Columba peers may show fewer reply previews on their
  end (they don't recognise `fields[0x30]`); future Columba
  versions are expected to add the same dual-tolerance.
• Same dual-tolerance idiom LXMF already uses for the str/bin
  signature variant per spec §5.6 — accept what comes in, emit
  a single canonical shape.
